## Core GEO Strategy (Sources 1–10)

1. **Search Engine Land** — "Generative engine optimization (GEO): How to win AI mentions"
   https://searchengineland.com/what-is-generative-engine-optimization-geo-444418
   *Key insight*: GEO is the practice of positioning your brand so AI platforms cite you. Content
   must be clear, direct, self-contained, and fact-based. Well-known brands don't automatically win
   — smaller publishers can compete with consistent topic ownership.

2. **Profound (tryprofound.com)** — "10-step framework for generative engine optimization"
   https://www.tryprofound.com/resources/articles/generative-engine-optimization-geo-guide-2025
   *Key insight*: E-E-A-T remains critical. Target 20–30 unique prompts per core topic.
   71%+ of Americans already use AI search to research purchases.

3. **Strapi** — "Generative Engine Optimization (GEO): Complete Guide 2025"
   https://strapi.io/blog/generative-engine-optimization-geo-guide
   *Key insight*: Content structure = versioned API contracts. Poorly structured content creates
   negative feedback loops — fewer citations reduce perceived authority, which lowers future
   extraction probability.

4. **Directive Consulting** — "A Guide to GEO Best Practices"
   https://directiveconsulting.com/blog/a-guide-to-generative-engine-optimization-geo-best-practices/
   *Key insight*: After ChatGPT's October 2025 entity update, establish 1 primary entity per page
   with 3–6 supporting entities linked to Wikipedia/Wikidata.

5. **Solutions Review** — "11 GEO Best Practices Every Technology Vendor Needs to Know"
   https://solutionsreview.com/marketing-automation/generative-engine-optimization-geo-best-practices-every-technology-vendor-needs-to-know/
   *Key insight*: GEO flips traditional SEO logic — AI engines favor sources that provide complete
   answers. Content effectiveness is measured by citation frequency, not traffic metrics.

6. **Firebrand Marketing** — "GEO Best Practices for 2026"
   https://www.firebrand.marketing/2025/12/geo-best-practices-2026/
   *Key insight*: PR is one of GEO's most powerful allies. LLMs heavily weigh third-party sources,
   expert commentary, and reputable citations. Recency and topical timing matter.

7. **Siege Media** — "Generative Engine Optimization (GEO): What It Is and How To Do It"
   https://www.siegemedia.com/strategy/generative-engine-optimization
   *Key insight*: "Best X" listicles are AI gold. GEO thrives on content that speaks to both
   direct user intent AND latent intent (related questions).

8. **Insightland** — "Generative Engine Optimization: everything you need to know for 2025"
   https://insightland.org/blog/generative-engine-optimization-everything-you-need-to-know-for-2025/
   *Key insight*: In GEO, there is no "link juice" — there is "trust embedding". 58% of users have
   replaced classic search engines with AI for product/service research (Capgemini).

9. **Webpyrix** — "Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) Guide 2025"
   https://webpyrix.com/blog/generative-engine-optimization-geo-guide-2025
   *Key insight*: Tools for GEO: GA4 for AI referral tracking, Semrush/Ahrefs for trending
   questions, Google Structured Data Markup Helper, AnswerThePublic.

10. **Profound — Benchmark Guide** — "10-Step Framework for GEO"
    https://www.tryprofound.com/guides/generative-engine-optimization-geo-guide-2025

    Track visibility, citation authority, and sentiment on a consistent cadence.

## GEO for Comparison & Lead Gen Sites (Sources 11–13)

11. **Omnius** — "GEO Industry Report 2025"
    https://www.omnius.so/blog/geo-industry-report
    *Key insight*: After implementing GEO, one company achieved 10% of organic visits from
    generative engines within 90 days, with 27% of that AI traffic converting to SQLs.
    AI visitors stayed 30% longer than Google visitors.
    AI search traffic converts 4.4× the rate of traditional organic search.

12. **The VC Corner** — "GEO & AEO: How to Get Cited by AI Search in 2026"
    https://www.thevccorner.com/p/geo-aeo-how-to-rank-when-ai-answers
    *Key insight*: Google ranks pages; AI cites statements. Zero-click is the default.
    AI citations compound — when ChatGPT cites your content, users associate your brand
    with expertise on that topic. "Best X" queries trigger AI responses >70% of the time.

13. **GreenBanana SEO** — "How to Get Your Website Cited by ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude and Perplexity"
    https://greenbananaseo.com/get-cited-in-chatgpt/
    *Key insight*: ~90% of ChatGPT citations come from sources outside the top 20 Google results.
    "Best Of" structured lists with semantic H2/H3 headers are the most cited format.
    Comparison tables and first-person reviews with personal experience perform best.

## Academic Research — Princeton GEO Paper (Sources 14–16)

14. **Princeton / arXiv** — "GEO: Generative Engine Optimization" (original paper)
    https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735
    Authors: Aggarwal, Murahari, Rajpurohit, Kalyan, Narasimhan, Deshpande
    Published: ACM SIGKDD 2024 (KDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining)
    *Key insight*: First formal GEO framework. GEO-bench = 10,000 diverse queries.
    GEO can boost visibility by up to 40% in generative engine responses.
    Keyword stuffing (classic SEO) does NOT work for GEO.

15. **SEO.ai** — "Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) and How to Optimize [Princeton Study]"
    https://seo.ai/blog/generative-engine-optimization-geo
    *Key insight*: The "Cite Sources" GEO method led to 115.1% visibility increase for sites
    ranked 5th in SERP. Sites ranked lower in SERP benefit significantly MORE from GEO.
    Domain-specific optimization is essential — different strategies for different content categories.

16. **Search Engine Land** — "GEO framework introduced in new research paper"
    https://searchengineland.com/generative-engine-optimization-framework-introduced-research-paper-435855
    *Key insight*: 9 GEO methods tested: Authoritative, Keyword Stuffing, Statistics Addition,
    Cite Sources, Quotation Addition, Easy-to-Understand, Fluency Optimization, Unique Words,
    Relevant Backlinks. Top performers: Statistics Addition and Cite Sources.

## LLM-Specific Citation Behavior (Sources 17–19)

17. **Qwairy** — "Perplexity vs ChatGPT: AI Citation Study (Q3 2025)"
    https://www.qwairy.co/blog/provider-citation-behavior-q3-2025
    *Key insight*: Analysis of 118,101 AI responses with 669,065 citations across 8 providers.
    Perplexity cites 2.76× more sources per response than ChatGPT (21.87 vs 7.92 avg).
    Only 11% of domains are cited by both ChatGPT AND Perplexity — platform-specific optimization
    is essential, not optional.

18. **Geolyze / Medium** — "How AI Engines Cite Sources: Patterns Across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity"
    https://medium.com/@shuimuzhisou/how-ai-engines-cite-sources-patterns-across-chatgpt-claude-perplexity-and-sge-8c317777c71d
    *Key insight*: Platform behaviors:
    - ChatGPT: cites only when browsing active; uses hyperlinks
    - Claude: never cites unless asked and source material is provided
    - Perplexity: cites by default; numeric footnotes; real-time retrieval
    - Google: source list appears with every AI Overview; inline refs not guaranteed

19. **Superprompt.com** — "AI Traffic Surges 527%: How to Get Cited by ChatGPT, Claude & Perplexity"
    https://superprompt.com/blog/ai-traffic-up-527-percent-how-to-get-cited-by-chatgpt-claude-perplexity-2025
    *Key insight*: Analysis of 400+ websites across 12 industries.
    Platform preferences: ChatGPT = depth, Perplexity = freshness, Claude = technical accuracy.
    Claude has highest session value ($4.56) despite sending the lowest volume of traffic.
    Sites with H2→H3→bullet structure are 40% more likely to be cited.
    AI referral sessions jumped 527% (Jan–May 2025).
